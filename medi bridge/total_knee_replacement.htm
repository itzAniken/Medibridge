Total Knee Replacement Surgery

Your guide to understanding the procedure and post-surgery care.

What is Total Knee Replacement Surgery?

Total Knee Replacement, also known as knee arthroplasty, is a surgical procedure to replace the damaged knee joint with an artificial implant. This surgery is often recommended for individuals with severe arthritis or injury who experience chronic pain and difficulty in mobility.

Procedure Duration

The surgery typically takes around 1 to 2 hours, depending on the complexity of the case. Hospitalization for recovery usually lasts for 3 to 5 days, followed by several weeks of physical therapy.

Precautions Before Surgery

  • Inform your doctor about any allergies or medications you are taking.
  • Stop taking blood-thinning medications as advised by your doctor.
  • Undergo necessary pre-surgery tests like X-rays, blood tests, and ECG.
  • Maintain a healthy diet and avoid smoking and alcohol before the surgery.

Post-Surgery Care

  • Follow a physical therapy program to regain strength and mobility.
  • Avoid high-impact activities and maintain a balanced diet.
  • Keep the surgical wound clean and monitor for signs of infection.
  • Use walking aids like crutches or walkers until advised otherwise.
